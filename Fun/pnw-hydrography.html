Hydrography of the Pacific Northwest

7/21/2014

Ever wonder what path a river takes? Or how much water flows through a stream on any given year? Check out this MapBox map I made with Tilemill which shows all the streams in the Pacific Northwest. If you mouse over a stream, a window will pop up in the top right showing the stream name (if it has one), and the estimated average flow rate in cubic feet per second (cfs). Now, of course, the winters are really wet here and the summers are really dry so these estimates average out the seasonal differences to give you a general pattern of flows.
